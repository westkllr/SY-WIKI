Submitted January 27, 2026 (University of Minnesota, same group as Sarkar et al.). Presents an eco-driving algorithm for EVs with multi-speed transmissions that co-optimizes vehicle longitudinal speed and powertrain gear selection simultaneously to maximize energy efficiency. Constraints from a connected-vehicle-based traffic prediction algorithm ensure traffic safety and smooth flow at signalized intersections in mixed traffic. The complex nonlinear mixed-integer problem is simplified to achieve real-time implementability. Demonstrates up to **11.36% energy reduction** over an 18 km drive. Relevant to areas 1, 3, and 4. Connects to [[rachavelpula-2026-personalized-ev-energy]] (personalization of driving profile vs. eco-optimization of powertrain operation) and [[data-driven-partitioning-energy-management]]. The multi-speed transmission co-optimization is an underexplored dimension in EV eco-driving — most work assumes single-speed EVs.
