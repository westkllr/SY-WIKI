## HIGH Relevance

### 1. ARC: Adaptive Robust Joint State and Covariance Estimation
**Authors:** Hadji-Thomas, Stirling, Forbes (McGill)
**arXiv:** [2606.20428](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20428) | eess.SY | June 2026
**Summary:** Unified framework that simultaneously rejects outliers and estimates measurement covariance — resolves a long-standing gap where robust estimators and joint estimators were mutually exclusive. Adaptive loss shape parameters updated online.
**Relevance:** HIGH — Area 5 (vehicle state estimation), Area 2 (adaptive control). Sensor fusion for vehicles frequently encounters outlier-corrupted measurements (tunnel GPS dropout, wet-road wheel slip, IMU shock).
**Wiki page:** [[hadji-thomas-2026-arc-adaptive-robust-estimation]]

### 2. Robust Koopman MPC with Sets Updates for Time Delayed Systems
**Authors:** Zhang, Yao, Xu, You, Hu (NUDT + Tsinghua)
**arXiv:** [2606.16485](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16485) | eess.SY | June 15, 2026
**Summary:** First Koopman MPC design addressing nonlinear time-delayed systems with online uncertainty set updates. Eliminates conservatism of fixed pre-computed uncertainty bounds by adapting sets based on current state/control.
**Relevance:** HIGH — Areas 1, 2, 4. Koopman-based linear MPC for inherently nonlinear EV powertrain dynamics, with CAN-bus time delays that are characteristic of production vehicle architectures.
**Wiki page:** [[zhang-2026-koopman-mpc-time-delayed]]

### 3. Data-driven Control with Real-time Uncertainty Compensation for Multi-Fuel Engines
**Authors:** Sarkar, Banerjee, Raju, Altiner, Sun, Kim, Kweon (U. Minnesota + U.S. Army Research Lab)
**arXiv:** [2606.16171](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16171) | eess.SY | June 15, 2026
**Summary:** GPR-based data-driven framework for combustion phasing control in multi-fuel CI engines. Introduces a pseudo-engine-speed variable to enable real-time adaptation without explicit fuel identification.
**Relevance:** HIGH — Areas 1, 3, 4. GPR uncertainty compensation for powertrain control; methodology directly transferable to hybrid powertrain energy management with fuel/load uncertainty.
**Wiki page:** [[sarkar-2026-data-driven-multi-fuel-engine]]

### 4. Neural Network-Based Virtual Wheel-Speed Sensor for Enhanced Low-Velocity State Estimation
**Authors:** Schäfke, Weber, Vagapov, Schweers, Seel, Ehlers (Leibniz University Hannover + IAV GmbH)
**arXiv:** [2605.12230](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12230) | eess.SY | May 2026
**Summary:** NN virtual sensor fusing wheel-speed and motor-speed signals; 85% error reduction vs. production sensor, 47% vs. zero-phase filter. Validated on Volkswagen ID.7. Accepted to IFAC World Congress 2026 (Busan).
**Relevance:** HIGH — Areas 1, 5. Neural network sensor fusion directly targeting EV low-velocity state estimation — a core challenge for ADAS/autonomous EV. IFAC acceptance in Busan is directly relevant to Korean research community.
**Wiki page:** [[schafke-2026-virtual-wheel-speed-sensor]]

### 5. Operator-Theoretic and Physics-Guided Sequence Modeling of Lithium-Ion Battery Voltage Dynamics
**Authors:** Labib, Rasool, Ahmed
**arXiv:** [2605.01693](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01693) | eess.SY | May 3, 2026
**Summary:** Compares DMDc (delay-embedded operator-theoretic) vs. physics-guided PatchTST transformer for Li-ion battery voltage modeling on 30 Ah cell HPPC data. DMDc wins under limited data; transformer offers more flexibility. OCV decomposition is the physics-guided component.
**Relevance:** HIGH — Areas 1, 4, 5. Battery voltage modeling is foundational for EV SOC/SOH estimation and energy management control.
**Wiki page:** [[labib-2026-battery-voltage-dmdc-transformer]]
